1. Customer Identification Program (CIP)
All clients must undergo identity verification prior to account activation.
Required documents include government ID, proof of address, and a valid taxpayer identification number.

2. Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Thresholds
Any cash transaction exceeding $10,000 must be flagged and reported.
Suspicious activity includes unusual wire transfers, rapid fund movement, and inconsistent trading behavior.

3. Know Your Customer (KYC)
Customers must be classified according to risk tiers:
- Low risk: predictable account activity
- Medium risk: foreign transactions
- High risk: politically exposed persons (PEPs) or high volume transfers

4. Trading Surveillance Rules
The following market abuse patterns must be monitored:
- Spoofing: placing orders with no intent to execute
- Layering: placing multiple orders to manipulate prices
- Insider Trading: trading based on non-public information

5. Margin & Risk Controls
Clients may not exceed approved margin limits.
Any breach of margin requirements triggers an automatic liquidation workflow.

6. Reporting Obligations
All suspicious activity must be submitted to the compliance department within 24 hours.
Regulated entities must file appropriate SAR (Suspicious Activity Reports).
